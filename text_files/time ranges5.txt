WVB & WHKY earn spots on the U SPORTS Top 10 List

Women's Volleyball:

The Mount Royal Cougars women's volleyball team hosted the University of Calgary Dinos this weekend during the Canada West quarter-finals, winning 3-0 (25-14, 25-21, 25-17) on Friday and 3-1 (25-21, 21-25, 25-17, 25-13) on Saturday. Following the back-to-back wins, the women claimed the 3rd spot on the U SPORTS Top 10 list once again.

Fifth-year outside hitter Haley Roe was the top scorer for the Cougars this weekend, with 13 kills Friday night and 18 kills Saturday against the Dinos. Ella Lawrence dug 13 balls Saturday to keep the Cougars on top and Nyadholi Thokbuom stopped 12 blocks between the two games. Quinn Pelland assisted 75 balls and had 12 digs to help her team clinch the win. 

The women will host the University of Manitoba Bisons in the Canada West semi-finals this weekend on Kenyon Court. The games will be played on March 3 at 6 p.m., March 4 at 5 p.m. and March 5 at 3 p.m. if needed. Come support your Cougars on their journey to nationals by getting your tickets here or watch all the action live on Canada West TV.  

Women's Hockey: 

The Mount Royal Cougars women's hockey team secured their spot in the Canada West Championship next weekend after they defeated the University of Alberta Pandas on the road. This win also earned the women's hockey program 9th spot on the U SPORTS Top 10 list. 

The women's team won Friday night 2-0, lost Saturday 1-0 and pulled out a 2-1 win in Sunday's game three to move on to the finals. Friday night's goals were scored by Tatum Amy and Athena Hauck. Sydney Benko and Laura Cook also had a pair in Sunday's game, all while Female Athlete of the Week, Kaitlyn Ross, stopped 26 shots on Friday, 17 on Saturday and 10 on Sunday.

The women will fly west to play the University of British Columbia Thunderbirds in the Canada West Championships Friday, March 3 at 7 p.m., Saturday, March 4 at 7 p.m. and Sunday, March 5 at 3 p.m. if needed. Watch the gold medal games live on Canada West TV.
